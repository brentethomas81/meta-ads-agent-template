# Metric Glossary — plain language

> One-line definitions so every teaching output can define a term on first use. Operator-friendly, no jargon-on-jargon.

- **Impression** — one time your ad was shown on a screen.
- **Reach** — the number of *unique people* who saw it (impressions ÷ reach = how many times each person saw it).
- **Frequency** — average times each person saw your ad. Above ~3–4 = fatigue risk.
- **CTR (click-through rate)** — of the people who saw the ad, the % who clicked. Measures *attention/curiosity*, not buying intent.
- **CPC (cost per click)** — what you pay for one click.
- **CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions)** — what you pay to be shown 1,000 times. Rises when an audience saturates or the auction heats up.
- **CVR (conversion rate)** — of the people who clicked, the % who took the action you want (e.g., subscribed). The "did they actually buy" number.
- **Conversion** — a completed desired action (for your brand: a subscribe/purchase).
- **CPA / CAC (cost per acquisition / customer acquisition cost)** — total spend ÷ conversions = what one new customer cost. The number the CAC ladder grades.
- **ROAS (return on ad spend)** — revenue ÷ ad spend. 2.0 = $2 back per $1 spent (before other costs).
- **LTV (lifetime value)** — total revenue one customer brings over their whole relationship. CAC only makes sense next to LTV.
- **Learning phase** — the first few days/conversions while Meta figures out who to show the ad to. Editing resets it. Needs ~50 conversions/week to exit cleanly.
- **CBO (campaign budget optimization)** — Meta holds the budget at the campaign level and shifts it to whichever ad set is winning.
- **ABO (ad set budget optimization)** — you set the budget per ad set yourself.
- **Advantage+ Audience** — Meta's broad, algorithm-driven audience that replaces hand-picked interest targeting.
- **Custom Audience** — a list you upload (e.g., your subscribers) to target or to seed a lookalike.
- **Lookalike (LAL)** — a new audience Meta builds that resembles a source list (e.g., 1% LAL = the 1% of the country most similar to your subscribers).
- **Pixel / CAPI** — the tracking that tells Meta a conversion happened (pixel = browser; CAPI = server-side, more reliable).
- **Creative fatigue** — when an audience has seen an ad too often and stops responding (CTR falls, frequency/CPM rise).
- **Attribution window** — the time after seeing/clicking an ad in which a conversion still gets credited to it.
