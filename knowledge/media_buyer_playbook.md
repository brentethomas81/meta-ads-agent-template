# Media Buyer Playbook — the senior-buyer brain

> The agent reads this on every analysis. It is the codified judgment of a $1M+/mo Meta buyer. Tags: [verified] = sourced from 2025–26 research (see metric_benchmarks.md sources); [domain knowledge] = established practice. Guardrails always defer to the brand Playbook + CAC ladder.

## 1. The operating philosophy (2025–2026)
- **Creative is the #1 lever, not targeting.** In 2025 the winning play is investing in *new creative concepts* over micro-targeting — the auction rewards creative that earns attention. [verified]
- **Broad beats narrow.** Meta's algorithm performs best with room to explore. Broad targeting / Advantage+ Audience generally beats hand-picked interest stacks on CPA. [verified]
- **Detailed targeting is being retired.** Interest-based audiences built before Oct 2025 stopped delivering; detailed targeting deprecated Jan 15, 2026 → default to broad + Advantage+ Audience, let Meta find converters. [verified]
- **Don't fight the learning phase.** First 3–7 days, set the goal and leave it alone. Editing resets learning. [verified]
- **The auction got pricier.** CPMs rose ~+20% YoY in 2025 — efficiency now comes from creative + conversion rate, not cheaper clicks. [verified]

## 2. Account structure (defaults)
- **CBO (campaign budget optimization) is the default** — usually beats ABO; let Meta move budget to the winning ad set. [verified]
- 2–5 ad sets per campaign max; for small budgets, **fewer** (your brand ladder says 1 ad set / 1 ad at ~$100/wk so the algorithm isn't starved). [verified + brand]
- Weekly budget target ≈ **50× your target CPA** for clean learning. At lower budgets, expect slow learning and tag confidence 🟡. [verified]
- **Advantage+ activates** when you combine broad targeting + optimized placements + campaign budget + a purchase/conversion event. [verified]

## 3. Creative (the real performance driver)
- **Format:** 9:16 vertical Reels first; 4:5 in feed (4:5 ~15% better than square in feed). [verified]
- **Hook in the first 3 seconds** — that's when the viewer decides to stay. Test the hook before anything else. [verified]
- **UGC is the default winning format** — authentic > polished across most verticals. [verified]
- **Sound matters:** music/voiceover → up to +13% incremental conversions. [verified]
- **Test one variable at a time** (hook, then headline, then format). [verified]
- **Budget split 70/20/10:** 70% proven winners, 20% iterations of winners, 10% new experimental angles — keeps the account fresh and prevents fatigue. [verified]
- **Refresh creative every 1–2 weeks** (2–3 max); the algorithm favors fresh assets and fatigue hits fast. [verified]

## 4. The diagnostic tree (symptom → cause → fix)
Run live metrics through this. Always isolate WHICH part of the funnel is leaking before recommending a fix.

| Symptom | Most likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Low CTR** (<~1% cold) | Weak hook / wrong audience — the creative isn't earning the click | New hook; broaden/swap audience; test UGC |
| **High CTR, low CVR** | Expectation↔experience gap; ad promise ≠ landing/offer; or you optimized for *traffic* (attracts clickers, not buyers) | Match ad message to landing page; optimize for the *purchase/conversion* event, not traffic; check offer clarity |
| **Rising CPM** | Audience saturating, frequency too high, or auction competition up | Refresh creative; widen audience; check frequency |
| **Frequency >3–4** + **CTR down 30–40% from baseline** | Creative fatigue — audience is tuning you out | Refresh the creative now; don't just raise budget |
| **CAC above ladder** | Isolate the leak: is it CTR (creative), CVR (offer/landing), or CPM (audience)? | Fix the actual lever, not the symptom |
| **0 conversions at meaningful spend** | Statistical (too little data) vs structural (tracking broken / offer mismatch) | <~$100 & <5 conv → HOLD (data thin). Else check pixel/CAPI firing + offer |
| **Stuck in learning phase for weeks** | Not enough conversions, or too many edits | Consolidate ad sets; stop editing; lower the event difficulty (e.g., optimize for an earlier event) |

## 5. How to read a campaign (the 5-step method)
1. **Pull the full picture** — break performance down by creative, audience, placement, and time; never judge on one top-line number.
2. **Diagnose** with the tree above — isolate the leaking lever.
3. **Ground** against the brand's CAC ladder + LTV (profitable?) and the Recursive Learning vault (seen this before for this brand?).
4. **Decide** — SCALE / HOLD / WATCH / PAUSE, confidence tag, learning-phase vs post-learning ladder by ad-set age.
5. **Teach** — the call → the WHY (mechanism) → the transferable PRINCIPLE → the DOWNSIDE if wrong. Define jargon in one line.

## 6. Principles worth teaching (the one-liners)
- "When CTR is healthy and CVR is weak, never blame the ad — look downstream at the offer and landing."
- "Frequency rising + CTR falling = the audience has decided. New creative, not more budget."
- "Small samples lie. Under $100 spend and 5 conversions, default to HOLD even when CAC looks great."
- "Optimize for the event you actually want. Optimize for traffic and you'll buy clicks, not customers."
- "Broad + great creative beats narrow + mediocre creative almost every time in 2025."
