# Metric Benchmarks — what "good" looks like (2025–2026)

> Reference ranges so the agent can say "your CTR is excellent / your CVR is the problem" against real data, not a vibe. Benchmarks are directional — the brand's own history (Recursive Learning vault) overrides these once it exists. All figures [verified] from the sources at the bottom; refresh ~quarterly and update the date.

**Last refreshed:** 2026-06-17

## Platform-wide medians (all objectives, 2025)
| Metric | Median | Read it as |
|---|---|---|
| CTR (link) | **2.19%** | Below ~1% cold = weak hook/audience; 2%+ = healthy; 3%+ = strong |
| CPM (cost / 1,000 impressions) | **$13.48** | Rising over time on the same audience = saturation/fatigue |
| CPC | varies | Traffic ≈ $0.70; Lead gen ≈ $1.92 |
| CVR (conversion rate) | **1.57%** | Ecommerce 1–2% is normal; lead gen runs much higher |
| CPA | **$38.17** | Only meaningful vs the brand's LTV / CAC ladder |
| ROAS | **1.93** | Below ~1 = losing money on ad spend alone |

## By objective
- **Traffic campaigns:** CPC ≈ $0.70. ⚠️ Traffic attracts clickers, not buyers — high CTR here is normal and does NOT predict purchases. (A Traffic-objective campaign can show high CTR but zero sales — it buys clicks, not buyers.)
- **Lead generation:** CPC ≈ $1.92, CVR ≈ 7.72%, CPL ≈ $27.66.

## Conversion-rate ranges by industry (lead ads, highest performers)
Fitness ~14.3% · Education ~13.6% · Employment/Training ~11.7% · Healthcare ~11.0% · Real Estate ~10.7%.
Ecommerce CVR is much lower: Food & Beverage ~2.0% (top) down to Electronics ~1.2% (bottom).

## CPM by industry (selected)
Beauty & Health highest (~$12.46) — competitive auction. Hardware & Automotive lowest (~$6.96).

## Year-over-year trend
CPMs up **~+20%** in 2025 — the auction is more expensive. Efficiency now comes from better creative and a tighter click→purchase funnel, not cheaper impressions.

## How to use these
1. Pull the campaign's actual CTR/CPM/CVR/CPA.
2. Compare to the row above to locate the leak (is CTR fine but CVR low? → downstream problem).
3. Translate to the brand's CAC ladder + LTV for the actual Scale/Hold/Pause call.
4. Once the Recursive Learning vault has 20+ closed conversions for a brand, prefer the brand's own numbers over these platform medians.
